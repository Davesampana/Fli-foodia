Region I (Ilocos Region)

Most Famous Dish in Region I

Pinakbet Ilocano

Walastik Pares is a version of Beef Pares created by Wilber Ramos made with a slightly thick sauce and instead of just the usual beef cuts, beef innards are used such as brains, intestines, bone marrow and tendons to name some.

How to Cook Walastik Pares?

Ingredients:

  • 8 round eggplants
  • 2 bitter gourd small
  • 1 bundle string beans
  • ⅔ cup squash
  • 8 pieces Okra
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • 2 Onions
  • 2 Garlic cloves
  • 1 Ginger small piece
  • Water
  • 1 lb Pork Belly
  • ¼ cup anchovy sauce
  • Salt & pepper to taste

Instructions:

For the Bagnet or Fried Pork Belly

  1. Place the pork belly in a deep pot and cover with water. Add the peppercorns and let it boil. Simmer for 20 minutes.
  2. Remove the boiled pork belly from the pot and let it cool.
  3. Heat oil in a frying pan. Gently add the pork belly and deep fry on medium heat until the pork turns crispy.
  4. Remove the pork belly from the pan and let it cool down.
  5. Once cool, slice the pork bagnet into bite-sized pieces and set aside.

For the Pinakbet

  1. Cut the round eggplants in half and cut the tomatoes and onions in chunks.
  2. Crush the garlic and slice the ginger in thin slices. Set aside.
  3. In a large pot bring water to boil. Add the anchovy sauce and wait for it to boil one more time.
  4. Add the vegetables: squash, okra, seedless bitter melon, eggplant, string beans, tomato, onions, garlic, and ginger.
  5. Simmer for 15 minutes until the vegetables are cooked.
  6. Add the sliced bagnet and mix well.
  7. Transfer to a serving plate and enjoy hot by itself or with a side of rice.
